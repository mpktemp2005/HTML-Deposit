e (Euler's number, ≈2.71828) is a special number that shows up whenever something grows continuously — a little bit is added back in constantly, and that new bit immediately starts growing too. This tool shows how e emerges from compound growth.

Each bar is $1 compounded n times over one period. More frequent compounding pushes the result higher, but it never goes past the dashed line — that ceiling is e.

n is how many times growth compounds over one period — e.g. n = 12 means monthly compounding, n = 365 means daily. Higher n means growth is added back in smaller, more frequent steps.

Formulas

  • e = lim (1+1/n)ⁿ
  • e ≈ 2.71828
  • d/dx eˣ = eˣ
  • n → ∞

lim ("limit") means: the value this expression settles at as n keeps growing, without n ever actually reaching infinity.
n → ∞ ("n approaches infinity") is what's changing in that limit — n climbing forever.
d/dx eˣ = eˣ means: at any point on the curve y = eˣ, the curve's slope (rate of change) equals the curve's own height. That self-matching property is unique to e.

What e means

e ≈ 2.71828182

Take $1 and compound growth 1 time(s) over one period. As you compound more and more often — daily, hourly, every instant — the result stops climbing much further and settles at e. It's the number continuous growth converges to, which is why it shows up anywhere something grows smoothly at a rate proportional to its own size.
