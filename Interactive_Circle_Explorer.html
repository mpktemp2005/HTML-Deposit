Radius is the distance from the center of the circle out to its edge. Every other measurement below is derived from it.

Formulas

  • C = 2πr
  • d = 2r
  • C = πd
  • A = πr²

r = radius (center to edge).
d = diameter, the distance straight across through the center — always twice the radius.
C = circumference, the distance all the way around the circle.
A = area, the space enclosed inside the circle.
π ("pi") = the ratio of circumference to diameter — the same fixed number, ≈3.14159, for every circle regardless of size.
r² means r multiplied by itself (r × r).

What π means

π ≈ 3.14159265

Lay the diameter (160) along the curved edge of the circle, over and over. It takes 3.14159 lengths to go all the way around — that ratio is π, and it's the same for every circle, no matter the size. The orange marks on the circle show where each diameter-length ends.
